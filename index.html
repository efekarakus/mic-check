Communication

In League of Legends, players need to share information with each other in order to kill opponents, or take objectives. Effective communication leads to players knowing which steps to take next, and thus win.

We recorded the voices of 3 of our players in a game of LoL, where each bar represents a second of the game. Our goal is to explore different aspects of interactions and show the potential of speech analysis in competitive video games.

Play

Distribution

The players talked on average for 5:17 minutes with the distribution being almost uniform.

In the previous section, we can observe the infamous top lane island as Fiora has the lowest amount of contribution during the laning phase. The player becomes more involved as the team starts grouping up and take down objectives.

Objective Control

While taking objectives, players should be coordinating the action. Furthermore, teammates should not interrupt each other to enable a fluid sequence of events.

In this section, we focus on events between 29:00 and 32:00. For example, Fiora kills Yasuo at 30:00 with the help of Hecarim. Although the two players were talking during the event, they also interrupted each other.

Interruptions

We have highlighted all the interruptions between at least two players during the match.

Fiora was interrupted for 98 seconds out of 344 seconds, therefore 28.5% of the time another player was talking concurrently. Hecarim was interrupted for 39.6%, and Zilean for 35.6% of the time!

Conclusion

There is so much to explore about how communication affects your in game performance! Maybe the volume of the players make a difference? Is there an optimal time to start discussing when to take Baron or Dragon?
