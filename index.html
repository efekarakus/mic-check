Communication

One of the intricacies of multiplayer esports such as League of Legends (LoL) is that the players need to communicate efficiently while playing the game in real time.

In this interactive, we explore what communication in a game of LoL looks like. We recorded the voices of 3 of our players, with each bar representing a second of the game.

Play

Distribution

The players talked on average 5:17 minutes. Which means that they talked ~14.2% of the game time. Surprisingly, we can observe that all the players seemed to talk roughly the same amount.

Fiora, is the shot caller of the team, therefore it does make sense that he had the most amount of input. However, the difference is not considerable.

Perhaps, in a team like Cloud9 where the team captain seems to have a significant impact this distribution would be highly skewed for Hai.
